01 —

What Is This Game?

Space Goats is a 2–5 player strategic card game about goat-piloted spaceships. Players build defenses, fire rockets into orbit, and race to either blow up every other ship or secretly stockpile enough Space Grass to colonize the void.

Each turn you draw, fire (or hold) your orbiting rockets, collect Grass, play cards to upgrade your ship, and clean up your hand. The game mixes aggressive combat with quiet resource management — ignore someone's Grass pile at your peril.

02 —

How to Win

Win Condition 1
Combat Victory
Be the last player with a ship that isn't destroyed. Blow everyone else out of the sky.
Win Condition 2
Colony Victory
At the end of your Resource Phase, if your personal Grass stockpile reaches 6 Space Grass cards, declare Colony Victory. Opponents have one last chance to Steal before it confirms.
03 —

Setup

  1. Separate the 5 Ship Cards and deal one face-up to each player.
  2. Separate all 18 Space Grass Cards and shuffle them into their own face-down Grass Pile. Place it in a shared area everyone can reach.
  3. Shuffle the remaining cards into the face-down Shared Pile (81 cards for 3–5 players; see the 2-Player Variant section for exceptions).
  4. Deal 5 cards from the Shared Pile to each player as their starting hand.
  5. Decide who goes first (suggestion: whoever most recently watched a space-related movie).
2-Player Duel? Before playing, each player attaches one Shield card to Slot 0 of their ship as starting equipment. See the 2-Player Variant section for the full rule differences.
04 —

Your Ship

Hull HP
2
Your ship absorbs 2 direct Hull hits before it is destroyed. Only rockets that bypass all your Shields hit the Hull.
Upgrade Slots
4
Attach Shields and Boosters to these slots. Maximum 4 attached cards at any time.
Orbit Zone Cap
2
You may hold up to 2 Standard Rockets in orbit at a time. Must fire before staging more if at cap.
Hand Limit
5
At end of turn, discard down to 5 cards. Choose what to keep carefully.
Key rule: Rockets only target Shields, never Boosters. If a ship has no Shields but still has Boosters in slots, the rocket bypasses the Boosters and hits the Hull directly.
Slot replacement: You may freely play into any open slot. To replace an occupied slot, you must first discard the card currently there — it cannot return to your hand. Gone is gone.
Ship Names (identity only — all ships are mechanically identical)
The Baaad Omen The Grazing Fury The Cud Bucket The Bleating Heart The Herd Ship Mentality
05 —

Turn Structure

1
Phase
Draw
Draw 1 card from the Shared Pile into your hand. If the Shared Pile is empty, shuffle the discard pile to form a new one first. Some Boosters let you draw an extra card here.
2
Phase
Orbit Resolution
For each Rocket in your Orbit Zone, choose to fire or hold:
  • Fire: Declare a target. The rocket hits one Shield, or the Hull if no Shields remain. Opens a Card Stack window — opponents may respond before it hits.
  • Hold: Rocket stays in orbit and carries over. You may keep holding to build pressure.
Skip this phase entirely if your Orbit Zone is empty.
3
Phase
Resource
Draw 1 card from the Grass Pile and place it face-up in your personal stockpile. If the Grass Pile is empty, skip this phase entirely.

After collecting, if your stockpile reaches 6 Grass cards, immediately declare Colony Victory. Opponents may respond with Steal cards before it confirms.
4
Phase
Play
Play any number of cards from your hand in any order. Options:
  • Shield → Attach to an open slot.
  • Booster → Attach to an open slot.
  • Rocket → Stage 1 per turn into Orbit Zone (max 2). Must fire one first if at cap.
  • Laser Rocket → Fire immediately at any target. Opens a Card Stack window.
  • Special Card → Open timing — play any time, on any turn.
  • Grass Spend → Return 2 Grass to bottom of Grass Pile, draw 1 from Shared Pile.
5
Phase
End
Discard down to 5 cards. You choose what to keep. Pass clockwise.
06 —

Card Types

🚀
Standard Rocket
19 cards
Stage into your Orbit Zone during Phase 4 (max 1 per turn). In Phase 2, choose to fire or hold. When fired, destroys one Shield on the target. If no Shields remain, hits the Hull. Boosters are never a valid target.
⚡
Laser Rocket
6 cards (3+ players only)
Bypass the Orbit Zone entirely — fire immediately when played during Phase 4. Hits one Shield or the Hull directly. Still opens a Card Stack window, so opponents may respond. Cannot be played on Turn 1.
🛡️
Shield
19 cards
Attach to an open slot. Absorbs one rocket hit, then is destroyed and discarded. Only Shields protect the Hull — Boosters do not. A ship is vulnerable the moment all Shields are gone.
⚙️
Booster
14 cards
Attach to a slot for a passive effect while it stays attached. Never destroyed by rockets — only Sabotage Special Cards can remove them. Replacing a Booster discards the old one (it does not return to hand). See the Booster Reference section for all 14 effects.
🌿
Space Grass
18 cards (separate pile)
Never drawn into your hand. Collected automatically from the Grass Pile during Phase 4. Kept face-up as your personal stockpile. Collect 6 to win. Alternatively, spend 2 Grass during Phase 4 to draw 1 card — a deliberate tradeoff that moves you further from Colony Victory.
07 —

Special Cards

Special Cards have open timing — play them on your turn or any opponent's turn. They go on the Card Stack when played and resolve when the stack is resolved. There are 5 subtypes, 23 cards total.
🛑
Intercept
5 cards
Destroy or redirect a declared rocket before it hits its target. Best played the moment a rocket is fired at you or an ally.
🌾
Steal
5 cards
Take 1 Grass card from a target opponent's stockpile. Essential for blocking Colony Victory — resolves before the win is confirmed.
💥
Sabotage
5 cards
Destroy any Shield or Booster currently attached to an opponent's ship. Knocks out one card of your choice from any slot.
🔧
Repair
4 cards
Recover one Shield or Booster from the discard pile and attach it to an open slot on your ship.
🚀
Launch
4 cards
Instantly stage a rocket from your hand into your Orbit Zone outside of Phase 4. Subject to normal Orbit Zone cap of 2.
08 —

The Card Stack

The Card Stack governs how actions resolve when players respond to each other. It opens whenever a rocket is declared firing or a Special Card is played. Any player may respond before resolution.
Example Resolution — Reads Top → Bottom
3RD (TOP)
Player A plays Sabotage → destroys Player B's Intercept
resolves first
2ND
Player B plays Intercept → but it's been Sabotaged, so it fizzles
fizzles harmlessly
1ST (BOT)
Player A's Rocket declared firing at Player B
resolves last — rocket hits
↑ STACK RESOLVES TOP → BOTTOM ↑
  • ◆Any player may add a Special Card on top of any stack event — not just the players directly involved.
  • ◆Each new card added opens another response window. Keep responding until all players pass.
  • ◆Once all players pass, resolve top to bottom. The most recently added card resolves first.
  • ◆Rocket targets are declared at firing — a rocket in the Orbit Zone has no target until its owner announces it is firing.
  • ◆If an effect is no longer valid when it resolves (e.g. the rocket it was meant to intercept was already destroyed), it fizzles — no effect, discarded harmlessly.
09 —

Space Grass

Collecting Grass

The Grass Pile is entirely separate from the Shared Pile. During Phase 3, draw 1 Grass card and place it face-up in front of you as your personal stockpile. If the Grass Pile is empty, skip Phase 3 entirely for that turn.

Colony Victory

The moment your stockpile reaches 6 Grass cards at the end of Phase 3, declare Colony Victory. Opponents may immediately play Steal cards in response. If your stockpile still reaches 6 after all Steals resolve, you win.

Grass Spend Ability

During Phase 4, you may return 2 Grass from your stockpile to the bottom of the Grass Pile to draw 1 card from the Shared Pile. This moves you further from Colony Victory — use it only when you need the cards badly.

Elimination & Grass

When a player is eliminated, their Grass stockpile goes to the bottom of the Grass Pile (not the discard). Their hand and all ship cards are discarded normally.

11 —

2-Player Duel Variant

Special Rules for 2-Player Games
  • No Laser Rockets. All 6 Laser Rocket cards are removed from the deck before shuffling. The Shared Pile contains 73 cards instead of 81.
  • Starting Shields. Before the game begins, each player attaches one Shield card to Slot 0 of their ship. These come from the deck — they are not shuffled in.
  • These rules apply only to games that start with 2 players. If a 3–5 player game eliminates players down to 2, these variant rules do not activate mid-game.
