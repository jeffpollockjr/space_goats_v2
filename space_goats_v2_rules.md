# Space Goats v2 — Player Rules

*A 2–5 player strategic card game of rockets, shields, and interstellar grazing.*

---

## The Goal

There are two ways to win:

- **Combat Victory** — Be the last player with a ship still flying.
- **Colony Victory** — Collect **6 Space Grass cards** in your personal stockpile. The moment you hit 6 at the end of your Resource Phase, you win — unless an opponent can steal from you first.

You don't have to pick one strategy. The best players do both.

---

## What's in the Box

The game uses **104 cards** split into three groups:

**Ship Cards (5)** — Dealt to players at the start. Never shuffled into a pile.

**The Shared Pile (81 cards, shuffled)** — The main draw pile. Contains:
- 19 Standard Rockets
- 6 Laser Rockets *(excluded in 2-player games)*
- 19 Shields
- 14 Boosters
- 23 Special Cards (Intercept, Steal, Sabotage, Repair, Launch)

**The Grass Pile (18 cards, shuffled separately)** — Only Space Grass lives here. It is never mixed with the Shared Pile.

---

## Setting Up

1. Find the 5 **Ship Cards** and deal one to each player, face-up in their play area.
2. Find all 18 **Space Grass** cards and shuffle them into their own face-down **Grass Pile**. Set it in the middle of the table.
3. Shuffle the remaining cards into the **Shared Pile**. *(For 2-player games, see the 2-Player Rules at the end.)*
4. Deal **5 cards** from the Shared Pile to each player's hand.
5. Pick a starting player however you like — the person who has most recently eaten something green goes first.

---

## Your Ship

Each player controls one ship. All ships are mechanically identical:

- **Hull HP: 2** — Your ship can absorb two Hull hits before it's destroyed.
- **4 Upgrade Slots** — Slots hold Shields and Boosters. You can have up to 4 cards attached at any time.
- **Orbit Zone** — A staging area in front of you where rockets sit before being fired. You can hold up to **2 rockets** here at once.

### How Damage Works

Rockets **only target Shields**. They cannot hit Boosters. Here's how a hit resolves:

- If you have at least one Shield attached, the rocket destroys it and stops there. The Shield is discarded.
- If you have **no Shields** (even if you have Boosters), the rocket hits your Hull directly, costing you 1 Hull HP.
- At 0 Hull HP, your ship is destroyed and you're eliminated.

**Boosters never protect your Hull.** Stack your Shields.

### Replacing Slot Cards

You can freely play a card into any **empty slot**. To replace a card already in a slot, you must **discard the current card first** — it cannot go back to your hand. Be careful: what you discard is gone.

## A Turn, Step by Step

Each player takes a complete turn before play passes clockwise. Every turn has **5 phases in order**.

---

### Phase 1 — Draw

Draw **1 card** from the Shared Pile into your hand.

If the Shared Pile is empty, shuffle the discard pile to form a new one, then draw.

> **Booster bonus:** *The Extra Nibble* lets you draw 2 cards instead of 1.

---

### Phase 2 — Orbit Resolution

For each rocket sitting in your Orbit Zone, decide: **fire it or hold it**.

**Firing:** Pick a target ship and announce it. The rocket hits their first Shield. If they have no Shields, it hits their Hull. After declaring, opponents have a chance to respond (see *The Card Stack* below) before the rocket actually lands.

**Holding:** The rocket stays put. No action required. You can hold rockets for as long as you like — building up pressure, waiting for the right moment.

**Important:** Your Orbit Zone holds a maximum of **2 rockets**. If it's full when Phase 4 comes around, you must fire at least one in Phase 2 before you can stage a new one.

If your Orbit Zone is empty, skip this phase entirely.

> **Booster bonus:** *Orbit Expander 3000* raises your cap to 3 rockets.

---

### Phase 3 — Resource

Take the **top card** of the Grass Pile and place it face-up in your personal stockpile in front of you.

If the Grass Pile is empty, skip this phase. No Grass is collected.

After collecting, count your stockpile. If you have **6 or more Space Grass cards**, immediately declare **Colony Victory**. Opponents get one chance to play a Steal card on you before it's confirmed — if your stockpile still meets 6 after any steals, you win.

> **Booster bonus:** *The Overgrown Engine* lets you collect 2 Grass cards instead of 1.

---

### Phase 4 — Play

Play any number of cards from your hand. You can play them in any order. Here's what each type does:

**Standard Rockets** — Move 1 rocket from your hand into your Orbit Zone. You can only stage **1 Standard Rocket per turn**, and only if your Orbit Zone isn't full. Staged rockets sit here until you fire them in a future Phase 2.

**Laser Rockets** — These skip the Orbit Zone and fire immediately. Pick a target and it hits right now. Opponents can still respond (via the Card Stack) before the hit lands. Laser Rockets can't be played on the very first round of turns.

**Shields** — Attach to one of your empty slots. Now you have one more layer of protection.

**Boosters** — Attach to a slot and gain a passive ability while it's there. Boosters are never destroyed by rockets — only Sabotage Special Cards can remove them.

**Grass Spend** — Return 2 Grass cards from your stockpile to the **bottom** of the Grass Pile, then draw 1 card from the Shared Pile. This is a deliberate trade-off: you're getting closer to Colony Victory by collecting Grass, but spending it buys you cards. Think carefully.

**Special Cards** — These can be played at any time (on your turn or anyone else's). See the Special Cards section below.

> **Booster bonuses:** *The Discount Grazer* reduces Grass Spend cost to 1. *Double Dip Drive* makes Grass Spend draw 2 cards instead of 1.

---

### Phase 5 — End

Discard cards from your hand until you have **5 or fewer**. You choose what to discard.

Your turn is over. Play passes clockwise.

---

## Special Cards

Special Cards are the game's wild cards. Unlike everything else, they have **open timing** — you can play them at any moment, on anyone's turn, including between responses in the Card Stack. Hold them in your hand until the right moment.

| Type | What It Does |
|------|-------------|
| **Intercept** | Destroy a rocket that has been declared firing before it hits. Play this the moment a rocket targets someone. |
| **Steal** | Take 1 Grass card directly from any opponent's stockpile. Can also be used to counter a Colony Victory declaration. |
| **Sabotage** | Destroy any one Shield or Booster currently attached to an opponent's ship. You pick the slot. |
| **Repair** | Recover one Shield or Booster from the discard pile and attach it to an open slot on your own ship. |
| **Launch** | Instantly stage a rocket from your hand into your Orbit Zone, outside of the normal Phase 4 window. Still subject to the 2-rocket cap. |

When you play a Special Card, it goes onto the **Card Stack** — other players may respond before it resolves.

---

## The Card Stack

When something important happens — a rocket fires, or someone plays a Special Card — a **response window** opens. This is the Card Stack.

### How It Works

1. An event is declared (a rocket fires, or a Special Card is played). That event sits at the bottom of the stack.
2. **Any player** may respond by placing a Special Card on top of the stack.
3. Every new card placed opens another response window. Players keep responding until everyone passes.
4. Once all players pass, the stack resolves **from top to bottom** — the most recently added card resolves first.

### A Quick Example

Player A declares they're firing a rocket at Player B.

- Player B plays *Wool Over Your Eyes* (Intercept) to stop it.
- Player A responds with *Cosmic Vandalism* (Sabotage) to destroy the Intercept first.
- Everyone passes.

Stack resolves top to bottom:
1. Sabotage resolves — the Intercept card is destroyed.
2. Intercept tries to resolve — but it's already gone. It **fizzles** harmlessly.
3. The rocket resolves — it hits Player B's ship.

### Fizzling

A card on the stack **fizzles** (does nothing and gets discarded) if its target is no longer valid by the time it resolves. Examples:
- An Intercept meant for a rocket that was already destroyed by something above it.
- A Steal targeting a player who now has 0 Grass.
- A Sabotage aimed at a slot that's now empty.

## Boosters — Full List

Boosters attach to a slot and are active as long as they stay there. They can't be removed by rockets — only Sabotage cards take them out.

| Booster | Effect |
|---------|--------|
| **The Extra Nibble** | Draw 1 extra card during Phase 1. |
| **Orbit Expander 3000** | Your Orbit Zone cap increases to 3. |
| **The Discount Grazer** | Grass Spend costs 1 Grass instead of 2. |
| **The Nosy Snout** | Once per turn, peek at the top card of the Shared Pile. |
| **The Freeloader** | Whenever any player uses Grass Spend, you also draw 1 card. |
| **Grief Wool** | When one of your Shields is destroyed, draw 1 card. |
| **The Generous Gate** | When you attach a Shield to your ship, draw 1 card immediately. |
| **The Panic Button** | Once per game, fire a rocket from your Orbit Zone at any time — even outside Phase 2. Then discard this card. |
| **The Overgrown Engine** | Collect 1 extra Grass during Phase 3. |
| **The Barbed Fence** | Opponents can only target your Grass stockpile with a Steal card if there are already **2 or more Special Cards** currently on the Card Stack. |
| **Double Dip Drive** | When you use Grass Spend, draw 2 cards instead of 1. |
| **The Periscope Horn** | Once per turn, peek at one opponent's full hand. |
| **The Silver Lining** | When one of your rockets is intercepted, draw 1 card as compensation. |
| **The Revolving Hoof** | When you discard a card from a slot to replace it, that card goes back to your hand instead of the discard pile. |

## When a Player Is Eliminated

When a player's Hull HP reaches 0:
- Their hand is discarded to the discard pile.
- All cards attached to their ship slots are discarded.
- Their personal **Grass stockpile is returned to the bottom of the Grass Pile** (not the discard pile — it re-enters circulation).
- They're out. The game continues with remaining players.

## Quick Reference

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Hand limit | 5 cards (discard at end of turn) |
| Slot limit | 4 cards attached to your ship |
| Orbit Zone cap | 2 rockets (3 with *Orbit Expander 3000*) |
| Rockets staged per turn | 1 Standard Rocket per Phase 4 |
| Colony Victory threshold | 6 Space Grass cards |
| Hull HP | 2 (ship destroyed after 2 Hull hits) |
| Grass Spend cost | 2 Grass → 1 card draw |
| Laser Rockets | Can't be played during the first round |
| Grass Pile empty | Skip Phase 3 entirely |
| Eliminated player's Grass | Returns to bottom of Grass Pile |

## 2-Player Duel Rules

When starting a game with exactly 2 players, three special rules apply:

1. **No Laser Rockets.** All 6 Laser Rockets are removed from the Shared Pile before shuffling. The Shared Pile has 73 cards.
2. **Starting Shields.** Before the game begins, each player attaches one Shield card to slot 0 of their ship. These Shields come from the deck before it's shuffled, so they're not part of the starting draw.
3. **Same Colony threshold.** The Colony Victory threshold is still 6 Space Grass cards.

Note: These rules only apply at game start. If you start with 3+ players and one gets eliminated, the 2-player duel rules do not kick in mid-game.

## Strategy Tips for New Players

- **Shield up before attacking.** A naked Hull is one rocket away from elimination. Get at least one Shield before picking fights.
- **Rockets in the Orbit Zone are a threat.** Even if you never fire, opponents have to respect the threat. Sometimes holding is better than shooting.
- **Watch the Grass Pile.** It only has 18 cards. Once it's empty, no one collects anymore. If someone is close to Colony Victory, you need to act fast.
- **Special Cards are most powerful reactively.** Holding an Intercept card can deter opponents from firing at you for several turns, just by them knowing you might have one.
- **The Barbed Fence is terrifying late game.** If you're close to a Colony Victory and you have it slotted, opponents need to jump through hoops just to slow you down.
- **Grass Spend is a tool, not a habit.** Spending your Grass for cards is great when your hand is empty, but it pushes you further from winning. Use it when you're stuck, not out of routine.
